Our History

Krispy Kreme was founded in 1937 in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, by Vernon Rudolph. Rudolph began selling doughnuts to local grocery stores and eventually opened his first retail store in 1937. The company's signature glazed doughnuts quickly became a hit with customers and Krispy Kreme began to expand throughout the southeastern United States.

In the 1950s and 1960s, Krispy Kreme continued to grow and expand, opening new stores in other parts of the country. In the 1970s and 1980s, the company faced financial difficulties and went through several ownership changes.

In the 1990s, Krispy Kreme began a period of rapid growth, expanding to new markets both in the United States and internationally. The company went public in 2000 and became one of the fastest-growing companies in the United States.

However, Krispy Kreme's rapid expansion and financial practices eventually led to a downturn in the early 2000s. The company faced allegations of accounting irregularities and its stock price plummeted. In 2005, Krispy Kreme brought in new leadership and began a restructuring process.

Today, Krispy Kreme is a global brand with over 1,000 stores in more than 30 countries. The company is known for its signature glazed doughnuts and its commitment to using high-quality ingredients.

Our Philosophy

Krispy Kreme's philosophy is to provide customers with the highest quality doughnuts and coffee possible, using only the finest ingredients and making them fresh daily. The company believes in delivering a joyful and inclusive experience to every customer that walks through its doors, and seeks to create an atmosphere of warmth, friendliness, and fun in every store. Krispy Kreme is committed to giving back to the communities it serves, and strives to be a good corporate citizen by promoting sustainability, ethical business practices, and philanthropy. Overall, Krispy Kreme's philosophy is centered around creating moments of happiness and connection through its delicious products and welcoming environment.
